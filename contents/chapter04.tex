\chapter{Discussion}

The Discussion chapter analyzes and interprets results, exploring their academic and practical implications, comparing them with prior studies, and underscoring the study’s value.

    
\section{Content Elements}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Result Interpretation: Explain the scientific or practical significance of the findings, addressing the research questions.
    \item Literature Comparison: Compare results with prior studies, highlighting uniqueness or consistency.
    \item Limitations Analysis: Honestly discuss the study’s constraints, such as sample size or methodological limitations.
    \item Impact and Applications: Explore the contributions to academia, industry, or other fields.
    \item Future Directions: Propose specific suggestions for further research or applications.
\end{itemize}

\section{Writing Tips：}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Structure the discussion by first interpreting results, then comparing with literature, and finally addressing limitations and impacts.
    \item Avoid exaggerating findings, maintaining objectivity and scientific integrity.
    \item Support arguments with specific data or literature citations to enhance credibility.
    \item If struggling to start, list how results align with research questions, then expand into analysis.

\end{itemize}
